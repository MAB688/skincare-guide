Specific Skin Issues

Listed below are a few common skin issues individuals experience. Although most of them are non-curable, over-the-counter products that reduce symptoms exist.


Acne

Most types of acne essentially occur when hair follicles under the skin become clogged. Skin oil, Sebum, and dead skin cells cause clogging when they stick together instead of shedding, congesting the pore. Individuals of all races and ages can experience acne.

Blackheads: Caused by clogged hair follicles that are open once they reach the skin top; interaction with oxygen causes darker discoloration.
Whiteheads: Caused by clogged hair follicle that stays under the skin, producing a white bump.
Pimples: Inflamed lesions capped by white or yellow pus-filled lesions.
Severe nodular acne or cystic acne: Deep, painful, pus-filled lesions.

Important Ingredients:
When looking for over-the-counter products to help your acne, the following ingredients benefit the breakdown of blackheads and whiteheads. Benzoyl peroxide kills bacteria and may decrease the production of sebum. Resorcinol may help breakdown blackheads and whiteheads. Salicylic acid helps break down blackheads and whiteheads, and helps reduce the shedding of cells lining the hair follicles. Sulfur helps break down blackheads and whiteheads. These ingredients can be found in cleansers, topical treatments or serums, and spot-treatment products.


Eczema

Skin swelling that causes dry, itchy skin and rashes, redness, and hyperpigmentation. Eczema is typically a long-lasting skin condition that operates in cycles and currently does not have an official cure. Over-the-counter treatments to lessen symptoms exist.

Important Ingredients:
When shopping for products to control itching, look for Antihistamines (such as Benadryl) or Colloidal oatmeal (found in lotions or body washes).to lessen symptoms, use oil-based moisturizing creams and products that are fragrance, alcohol, scents, and dyes free.


Rosacea

Various subtypes; main and most common symptom is small, red, pus-filled bumps on the skin. Bumps tend to arise during flare-ups, which occur in time period cycles.

Subtype One: Erythematotelangiectatic rosacea (ETR); facial redness, flushing, and visible blood vessels.
Subtype Two: Papulopustular rosacea; acne-like breakouts.
Subtype Three: Rhinophyma; thickening of the skin on the nose. Typically paired with another subtype.
Subtype Four: Ocular rosacea; centered around eye area.

Important Ingredients:
No cause or cure currently exists for Rosacea, but gentle cleansers and oil-free, water-based skin products can help relieve symptoms. Try to avoid products with alcohol, menthol, witch hazel, and exfoliating agents.

Psoriasis

A Chronic autoimmune condition that causes buildup of cells, leading to scaly, sharply defined skin patches; darker.complexions may also experience dark brown or purplish skin patches. No cure currently exists for psoriasis, but symptoms occur in cycles. These patches are commonly found on the scalp, elbows, knees and lower back. Potential symptom triggers to avoid include stress, alcohol, or injures (i.e. cut or scrape).

Important Ingredients:
Ingredients to look for in available over-the-counter topical treatments: Mild topical corticosteroids and retinoids, Salicylic acid, Moisturizers.
